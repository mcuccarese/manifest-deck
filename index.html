The next generation is multimodality and virtualization.

02 · The gap

Better models. Harder, more context-dependent validation.

Data production and AI capabilities in biology and chemistry are accelerating. As they bypass easily-predicted activity and properties, the validation that remains is increasingly context-dependent and harder to plan. Status-quo logistics and decision architecture cannot keep pace.

As models improve, two backpropagation gaps widen.

(1) Model validation
(2) Outcome prediction

Orthogonal validation

The better a predictive model is from a scaled platform, the less it will need to be confirmed with the same setup. A good model can predict out of training distribution; that’s where validation happens.

e.g.

  • Novel target binding prediction → new binding assay
  • Activity in new patient context → new translational assay

Right-shifted validation

We will be virtualizing biology at greater and greater complexity. Predicting outcomes at far later stages. Drug discovery programs will be running lead-op-like experiments to validate entirely in-silico-derived hypotheses.

We can completely change our concept of how many advanced programs a company can simultaneously run, if we set up our infrastructure for this future.

03 · The bet

Running the most informative experiments is solvable through coupled simulations.

The latency around running specific, program-decisive experiments comes from two human-bound steps: enumerating the right experiments and executing them across a moving resource map. Both can be solved by simulation, if the right two simulations are coupled.

04 · The architecture

Dual-simulation hybridization. Maximize decisions, minimize latency.

A next-generation digital twin of operations, fused with an outcomes simulation. The twin projects timelines for every candidate experiment in current resource state; outcomes ranks them by impact. The same loop forecasts what a new dataset, model, or instrument would shave off the next stage gate, not a benchmark score. One loop. Many more decisions per unit time.

The next decision was already committed during simulation. When the experiment realizes, it fires with Δt = 0. Portfolio-wide, this collapses the largest latency in current-generation drug discovery: the wait for a committee to convene after data returns.

05 · Semantic harmonization

One way to feed the pipeline.

LLM agents offer a powerful means for synthesizing cross-modal explanation, but are contaminated by conventional understanding, biasing analysis. One approach: build causal biological conviction from semantic harmonization of context-aware signal fragments across many data verticals.

Literature-explainable

“Target X is identified in STK11-mutant lung cancer. KO in a panel of lung cell lines and investigate cell death.”

Conventional next step in validation.

Data-explainable

“Target X is identified in STK11-mutant lung cancer. Perturb-seq atlases show KO in T has strong enrichment with the STK11 LoF vs WT transcriptional delta in patient tumors. KO the target in allogenic T-cells with an STK11 mutant tumor line.”

Test is based on a patient-informed hypothesis, not simply advancing to the typical next step.

07 · Tracking

Simulation addresses lagging indicators and enables forward projection.

Every decision at every moment is measured against well-informed models of industry expectations and our own predictions. Projected duration vs. realized. Predicted success vs. realized. The system learns to forecast itself.

Rebuild the entire discovery suite to complement the agentic revolution.

  1. Rapidly build a high-impact, deeply-validated portfolio through simulation-optimized execution and zero-latency decision-making.
  2. Every operational decision flows through the twin first. No experiment, CRO contract, instrument purchase, or hiring decision is committed without the simulation running it. Spend is a simulation output, not a committee output.
  3. Prospectively model strategic decisions and technological advancement.
